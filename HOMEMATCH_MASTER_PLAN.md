## PART 1: THE BUSINESS DECISION (COMMITTED)

### Chosen Monetization Path: TECH FEE MODEL (Path 2)

**Decision:** Charge agents/brokerages a flat fee per qualified tour lead. Do NOT collect commission splits.

**Why this path wins for the first 90 days:**
1. No brokerage license required to launch immediately
2. Revenue is predictable per-lead (not dependent on 60-90 day close cycles)
3. Avoids RESPA commission-splitting risk entirely
4. Agents/brokerages understand "pay per lead" — it's familiar
5. Faster cash flow: collect on tour verification, not closing

**Pricing structure:**
- **Months 1-2 (Proving):** First 3 referrals FREE per brokerage. This is your trust-building tool. You need closings to prove the funnel works.
- **Month 3+:** $150 per verified tour lead (buyer showed up, agent confirmed). This is below Zillow Premier Agent CPL in similar markets ($200-400+).
- **Optional premium:** $250 per "closing-ready" lead (pre-approved buyer with shortlist + readiness score 70+).

**Why NOT commission splits (Path 1) yet:**
- Requires licensed broker partnership or your own license
- Indiana requires real estate broker license to collect referral fees (IC 25-34.1)
- Adds 30-90 day setup time before any revenue
- Save this for Month 6+ after proving the funnel converts

**Fallback pivot (Month 6+):** If tour lead volume is strong, partner with a licensed IN broker to unlock referral fee revenue (25-35% of agent commission). This 10x's revenue per transaction but requires legal setup.

### Revenue Model Math

**Conservative scenario (90 days):**
- Month 1: 0 paid leads (all free tier, building pipeline)
- Month 2: 5-10 paid leads x $150 = $750-1,500
- Month 3: 15-25 paid leads x $150 = $2,250-3,750
- **90-day revenue: $3,000-5,250**

**Path to $1M ARR:**
- Need ~555 paid leads/month at $150 = $83K/month = $1M/year
- OR ~278 leads/month at $300 (closing-ready tier)
- At scale across 5 markets: ~55 leads/market/month — achievable
- **Fishers/Carmel alone could support 30-50 leads/month** (active market, ~200 transactions/month in Hamilton County)

## PART 3: COMPLIANCE DECISION TREE

### What You Can Do NOW (No License Required)

1. **Charge agents a flat fee for qualified leads** — This is a marketing/advertising service, not a referral fee. You are selling qualified buyer introductions, similar to Zillow/Realtor.com.
   - Key requirement: The fee must be for the SERVICE (lead generation, qualification, matching), not tied to whether a transaction closes
   - Structure: "Platform access fee" or "qualified tour lead fee"
   - Amount: Fixed dollar amount, NOT a percentage of commission

2. **Charge buyers a subscription** ($19/month premium) — This is a consumer software subscription. No license issues.

3. **Provide tools and information** — Budget calculators, match scoring, market data, checklists. These are informational tools, not real estate advice.

### What Requires Legal Verification BEFORE Doing

**MUST verify with an Indiana real estate attorney before:**

1. **Collecting any fee tied to a real estate transaction closing**
   - Question for attorney: "Can a non-licensed tech company charge agents per qualified lead in Indiana without a broker license, if the fee is a flat marketing fee unrelated to the transaction?"
   - Expected answer: Yes, if structured as a marketing/advertising service
   - Risk if wrong: Unlicensed practice of real estate

2. **Referral fee arrangements with brokerages**
   - Indiana Code IC 25-34.1 governs real estate licensing
   - Referral fees (% of commission) generally require a licensed broker
   - Question for attorney: "What is the minimum licensing requirement to receive referral fees from Indiana brokerages?"
   - Options: (a) Get a referral-only broker license, (b) Partner with a licensed broker who holds the referral relationship

3. **Using the word "referral" in your agreements**
   - Safer language: "marketing lead," "qualified buyer introduction," "tour request"
   - Avoid: "referral fee," "commission split," "cooperative compensation"

4. **RESPA considerations (for lender partnerships)**
   - If you refer buyers to lenders: RESPA Section 8 prohibits kickbacks for referrals of settlement services
   - Question for attorney: "Can we receive marketing fees from lenders for buyer introductions?"
   - Safe approach: Lender pays for advertising placement on your platform (like banner ads), not per-referral

### The Compliance-Safe Agreement Structure

```
AGREEMENT TITLE: "Qualified Buyer Marketing Services Agreement"

NOT: "Referral Agreement" or "Commission Sharing Agreement"

KEY CLAUSES:
1. HomeMatch provides marketing services: buyer qualification, matching, and tour request routing
2. Brokerage pays a flat fee per qualified tour lead ($150)
3. Fee is for the marketing service, regardless of transaction outcome
4. HomeMatch does not provide real estate advice or brokerage services
5. HomeMatch is not a party to any real estate transaction
6. Either party may terminate with 30 days notice
```

### Automated Status Checks (Anti-Ghost System)

**Weekly automated flow:**
1. Every Monday at 9am: System checks all leads with status != "closed" and status != "lost" and last update > 7 days ago
2. Sends agent an email: "Quick status check on [Buyer Name] (Lead HM-2025-001). Please update: [link to dashboard]"
3. Sends buyer a text/email: "Hi [Name], just checking in on your home search! Has your agent scheduled a tour? [Yes/No buttons]"
4. If buyer says "no" and agent hasn't updated in 14 days: Flag as "unresponsive agent" and consider reassignment
5. If buyer says "yes" + tour happened: Send agent payment invoice

**Buyer-side verification (critical for preventing ghost):**
- After a tour is marked "completed" by agent, buyer gets a confirmation text: "Did you tour [address] with [agent name]? [Yes/No]"
- Both parties must confirm for the tour to be "verified" (triggers payment)
- This dual-confirmation prevents agents from claiming fake tours AND prevents agents from ghosting real ones

## PART 8: HARDENING AGAINST RISKS

### Risk 1: Slow Agent Adoption

**Mitigation:**
- Free first 3 leads removes all risk for agents
- Focus on ONE brokerage first (get the managing broker bought in, then all agents follow)
- Show agents real buyer data: "This buyer has a 78 readiness score, $425K budget, is pre-approved, and wants to see 3 specific homes in Fishers. When was the last time Zillow gave you that?"
- Create a "league table" — agents who close leads get priority routing

### Risk 2: Low Tour-to-Close Conversion

**Mitigation:**
- Qualification gate: only route buyers with readiness score 50+ to agents
- Pre-approval question is mandatory before tour request
- Timeline filter: prioritize "ASAP" and "1-3 months" over "Just browsing"
- Follow up: automated check-ins keep the pipeline warm
- Accept that 5-10% tour-to-close is normal; the business works on volume

### Risk 3: Data Freshness/Credibility

**Mitigation:**
- RapidAPI Realty data updates daily (MLS-sourced)
- Add "last updated" timestamp to every listing
- Show "days on market" prominently (proves freshness)
- Market data files: update monthly from Redfin Data Center
- When a listing is stale or sold, show "This home may no longer be available" gracefully

### Risk 4: Compliance (Referral Fees)

**Mitigation:**
- Start with tech fee model (no commission splitting)
- Use "marketing services" language, never "referral"
- Get attorney review of agreement before first paid lead
- Keep detailed records of all services provided (qualification, matching, etc.)
- Don't give real estate advice (already have AI disclaimers)

### Risk 5: Tracking Leakage (Agent Ghosting)

**Mitigation:**
- Dual confirmation system (buyer + agent must confirm tour)
- Formal agreement with BROKERAGE (not individual agent) — managing broker is accountable
- 12-month non-circumvention clause
- Buyer relationship: we stay in touch with buyer directly, so we know if they bought
- Invoice at tour verification (not at closing) — faster revenue, harder to ghost
- Nuclear option: if brokerage ghosts payment, pause all new leads to that brokerage
